# TimeKeep — Flagship Product Film

**Director's document. All decisions flow from this.**

Runtime: 1:45 – 2:00  
Format: Vertical-first (9:16 mobile), horizontal cut optional  
Tone: Calm. Cinematic. Operational.

---

## The Brief

This film has one job: make the viewer feel what it's like to use software that doesn't get in the way.

Not "here are the features."  
Not "look what I built."

The feeling is: *this software belongs here.*

The emotional through-line is restraint. Every decision — shot duration, typography timing, sound texture, cut rhythm — serves that restraint. The product communicates confidence by never overselling itself.

---

## Narrative Structure

The film moves through five emotional beats. These are not acts — they blend into each other.

**1. Friction** *(0:00 – 0:22)*  
The world before. Operational pressure. Schedule chaos. The small frictions of coordinating people.

**2. Silence** *(0:22 – 0:30)*  
A breath. The transition. The reveal.

**3. Clarity — Employee** *(0:30 – 1:05)*  
The product in the hands of the person it was built for. Effortless. Fast. Real.

**4. Control — Admin** *(1:05 – 1:35)*  
The same calm from the other side. Creating order without friction.

**5. Resolution** *(1:35 – 2:00)*  
The product at rest. Quiet confidence. The tagline. Fade.

## Scene-by-Scene Breakdown

---

### Scene 1 — Friction *(0:00 – 0:22)*

**Story:** The weight of coordinating a small operational team. Not dramatic chaos — quiet friction. The kind that accumulates.

**Shots:**

1. **0:00 – 0:04**  
   Extreme close-up. A paper schedule pinned to a corkboard or clipboard. Handwritten. A name crossed out in pen. Another written above it. The light is warm but the image feels effortful.  
   *No music yet. Ambient room tone only.*

2. **0:04 – 0:08**  
   Phone screen. A group chat thread — shift times buried in messages. Thumb scrolls slowly upward. Can't find what they're looking for.  
   *Shallow depth of field. The background is softly blurred.*

3. **0:08 – 0:12**  
   Close-up. A hand writes a time on a sticky note. Presses it to a surface. Already feels temporary.  
   *Sound: soft pen on paper.*

4. **0:12 – 0:17**  
   Medium shot. Someone glances at their phone between tasks — not clocked in, unsure if they should be. A small hesitation. They pocket it.  
   *The hesitation is the point.*

5. **0:17 – 0:22**  
   Wide. End of a shift. Someone leaves. Nobody recorded it.  
   *Ambient fade. Sound drops to near silence.*

**Tone:** Unhurried. Not melodramatic. The friction is recognizable, not exaggerated.

---

### Scene 2 — Silence & Reveal *(0:22 – 0:30)*

**Story:** A single breath. Then the product appears.

**Shots:**

6. **0:22 – 0:26**  
   Black frame. Hold.  
   *Complete silence. Three beats.*

7. **0:26 – 0:30**  
   A phone face-down on a surface — warm stone countertop, wood, or concrete. A hand picks it up. Turns it over. The screen wakes.  
   *The screen glows: TimeKeep login. Warm `#faf9f7` background. Employee names.*  
   *Sound: a single, quiet ambient tone. Warm. Not a sting — a breath.*

**Direction note:** The reveal is the screen waking, not a product logo appearing. The product announces itself through use.

---

### Scene 3 — Clarity: Employee Experience *(0:30 – 1:05)*

**Story:** The product in real hands. PIN login through to clock-out. Every interaction feels immediate and unhurried at the same time.

**Shots:**

8. **0:30 – 0:38** — **Login: Name Selection**  
   Over-the-shoulder. Thumb scrolls the name list. Selects. The tap registers instantly — no lag, no animation delay.  
   *Macro close-up on the thumb making contact with the screen.*  
   *Sound: subtle, soft tap sound design.*

9. **0:38 – 0:46** — **PIN Entry**  
   Front-facing angle, shallow DOF. Four digit buttons tapped. The fourth digit — instant auth, instant redirect. No submit button.  
   *Shot captures the physical confidence of the interaction — no hesitation.*  
   *Sound: four soft, distinct taps. Slightly lower tone on the fourth.*

10. **0:46 – 0:54** — **Dashboard: Today's Shift**  
    The dashboard appears. Today's shift — large, readable time display. The phone is held naturally in one hand, thumb near the clock-in button. The UI communicates everything before the viewer reads anything.  
    *Typography treatment: the time is the hero element. Nothing competes with it.*

11. **0:54 – 1:00** — **Clock In**  
    One tap. The button state changes. An elapsed timer appears. The moment is immediate and unceremonious — that's the point.  
    *Capture the button's `active:scale-[0.97]` press feedback in slow motion. The tactile response of well-built software.*

12. **1:00 – 1:05** — **Schedule View**  
    Swipe to schedule. The week is readable instantly. Today is anchored. The phone tilts slightly — still held one-handed.  
    *Hold on this. Let the UI breathe.*

**Typography overlay during Scene 3:**  
Appears once, briefly, at the center of a dark transition frame between shots.

```
tap once.
clocked in.
```

White. SF Pro or Inter. Large tracking. Holds for 1.5 seconds. Fades.

### Scene 4 — Control: Admin Experience *(1:05 – 1:35)*

**Story:** The same calm from the management side. Creating structure without complexity.

**Shots:**

13. **1:05 – 1:12** — **Admin Dashboard**  
    Admin opens the app. The overview card: staff count, active shifts, who's clocked in. Scannable in one glance.  
    *Shot from slightly above, phone resting on a surface. Both hands visible — admin has more time than the employee.*

14. **1:12 – 1:20** — **Schedule View + New Shift Modal**  
    Admin taps the schedule. Weekly view — clean grid. Taps to create a new shift. The bottom sheet rises from the floor of the screen on its deceleration curve. The drag handle sits at the top.  
    *Capture the sheet-up animation clearly. This is the craft moment.*  
    *Slow the playback to 80% here so the animation reads.*

15. **1:20 – 1:27** — **Time Select**  
    Filling the shift form. The TimeSelect picker — iOS wheel appears. Native, immediate, correct. The time is set in one gesture.  
    *Close-up on the wheel picker in motion. Tactile.*

16. **1:27 – 1:32** — **Template Application**  
    Admin taps Apply Template. The week fills. A full week of shifts appears in a single action.  
    *Wide shot of the schedule populating. Let the viewer register what just happened.*

17. **1:32 – 1:35** — **Time Entries**  
    Quick cut. Time entries list — clock-in, clock-out, duration. Clean. No clutter. The admin reviews without effort.  
    *Hold briefly. Fade transition into Scene 5.*

**Typography overlay during Scene 4:**  
One line, center frame, dark background.

```
schedule set.
four seconds.
```

---

### Scene 5 — Resolution *(1:35 – 2:00)*

**Story:** The product at rest. Software that earned its place.

**Shots:**

18. **1:35 – 1:42**  
    Slow close-up. The dashboard — clocked in, timer running. The phone rests against a surface, screen visible. The elapsed time ticks. Someone is working.  
    *No interaction. Just the product existing in the world, doing its job.*

19. **1:42 – 1:48**  
    Pull focus from the phone to the background — a workspace, the suggestion of work being done. The software recedes. The work is the point.  
    *This is the only wide shot in the film.*

20. **1:48 – 2:00** — **End Card**  
    Slow fade to near-black. Then:

    ```
    TimeKeep
    ```

    Pause. Three seconds. Then beneath, smaller:

    ```
    Software shaped around real work.
    ```

    Hold five seconds. Fade to black.

    Minimal end card — horizontal rule, three items:
    - GitHub repository URL
    - Deployment URL  
    - Your name

    *Nothing else. No call to action.*

## Camera Direction

**Equipment philosophy:** Shoot on whatever gives you the sharpest image and the most control over depth of field. An iPhone 15 Pro on a small gimbal is sufficient. A mirrorless with a 50mm prime is better. This is not a production about equipment.

**Depth of field:** Shallow throughout. Background should dissolve. The phone and the thumb and the screen are always sharp. Everything else is texture.

**Movement:** Minimal. When the camera moves, it moves slowly and with intention:
- Slow push-in on static UI shots
- Slight handheld drift on real-world shots (not jitter — breathing)
- Pull-focus on the final wide shot only

**Angles for phone UI:**
- Over-the-shoulder for employee interactions
- Top-down (30° off vertical) for admin — more deliberate, more space
- Macro side-on for modal animations and button interactions

**Frame rate:** Shoot at 60fps. Edit at 24fps. This gives you the option to slow down any interaction to 80% without feeling artificial.

**Light:** Soft, directional, warm. A window with diffusion, or a single soft box. No ring lights, no RGB, no artificial warmth. The `#faf9f7` background on screen should feel continuous with the physical environment.

**Physical environment:** A real workspace. A countertop, a desk, a table. Not a clean studio void. The product lives in a real place.

---

## Motion & Transition Direction

**Cuts:** The default is a direct cut. No transitions unless they're motivated.

**Motivated transitions:**
- The silence cut at 0:22 — hard cut to black, hold
- Typography cards — brief fade in/out, not a wipe or slide
- End card — slow fade from the final shot

**Slow motion:** Use only twice:
1. The clock-in button press (Scene 11) — 80% speed to show the `active:scale` press feedback
2. The bottom sheet rising (Scene 14) — 80% speed to let the animation read properly

**Match cuts:** Look for opportunities between scenes — the hand picking up a phone in Scene 2 can match-cut to the hand picking up the phone in Scene 7. The theme of *the same gesture, different outcome* is available here.

**Speed ramp:** One moment only — from real-time to slight slow-mo as the clock-in button is tapped. The ramp happens on contact. It should feel like the product responding to touch, not a VFX choice.

## Sound Design

Sound is half the film. Treat it with the same attention as visuals.

**Scenes 1–2: Pre-product world**
- Room tone only — ambient, slightly hollow
- Pen on paper: real recorded, not stock
- Group chat notification: muted, small
- Silence from 0:22 – 0:26: complete. Mix this aggressively. No background hum.
- The ambient tone that enters at 0:26 with the phone reveal: a single sustained note, warm, low, not a sting

**Scenes 3–4: Product in use**
- Interface taps: real device sounds, not designed — record them from the actual app
- The four PIN taps: slightly differentiated, final tap has a fractionally lower pitch and longer decay
- Clock-in: a soft, definitive sound — not celebratory, just complete
- Modal sheet rising: no sound. Let it be silent and elegant.
- Timer ticking: no sound. Silence is the right answer here.

**Scenes 4–5: Resolution**
- Music fades to near-silence by 1:42
- The final sequence (1:42 – 2:00) is near-silence, then complete silence before the end card text appears

**Music:**
- Enters at 0:26 with the product reveal
- Single instrument or sparse electronic texture — piano, Rhodes, or minimal ambient synthesis
- No percussion until Scene 3, and even then: a soft, measured pulse, not a beat
- By Scene 4 the music should feel like it's settling, not building
- The film does not end on a crescendo. It ends quietly.

**References:** Ólafur Arnalds' ambient work. Jon Hopkins' quieter moments. Max Richter's lighter pieces. The texture of those, not the emotional scale.

## Typography Direction

**Typeface:** SF Pro Display (if on Mac — free via Apple), Inter, or Neue Haas Grotesk. These are the same families used in the product. Continuity between UI type and title cards is intentional.

**Weight:** Light or Regular for sentences. Medium for the product name at the end.

**Color:** White on dark. Never colored. Never with a drop shadow.

**Size:** Large. The typography should feel confident, not footnote-sized.

**Tracking:** Generous. These lines should breathe.

**Timing:** Typography appears slowly (250ms fade). Holds longer than feels comfortable. Disappears cleanly (150ms fade). The viewer should read it twice.

**Lines used in the film:**

| Timecode | Text |
|----------|------|
| ~0:54 | `tap once. clocked in.` |
| ~1:27 | `schedule set. four seconds.` |
| 1:48 | `TimeKeep` |
| 1:51 | `Software shaped around real work.` |

That is all the text in the film. Every word is load-bearing.

**The product name:** Set in Medium weight. Track it out slightly — the letters should not feel compressed. It appears alone first, holds, then the tagline appears beneath it in Light weight, smaller.

## UI Capture Strategy

**Priority screens (must capture):**

1. Login — name selection list (warm background, stone type, clean list)
2. PIN keypad (4 digits, no keyboard)
3. Employee dashboard — shift card with time, clock-in button prominent
4. Clock-in active state — button changed, elapsed timer running
5. Employee weekly schedule — today anchored, clean grid
6. Admin new shift bottom sheet — drag handle, modal rising, TimeSelect wheel open
7. Admin schedule — weekly grid with multiple employees and shifts

**Capture method:**
- Record real interactions on a physical device — not screen recording from simulator
- Use an external camera pointing at the screen for the tactile shots (thumbs, close-ups)
- For clean UI-only moments: screen record at 60fps, then composite onto device mockup in post if needed
- The sheet animation (Scene 14) must be captured live — the 240ms cubic-bezier curve will not reproduce correctly in a simulated environment

**What not to show:**
- Error states
- Empty states
- Settings pages
- Any loading indicators
- Any UI element that communicates "in progress"

The product in the film is always working. It is always calm.

## Editing Philosophy

The editor's job is to be invisible.

**Pacing:** Slower than feels natural. Most shots should hold slightly longer than comfortable. The film should never feel rushed. Premium products are not in a hurry.

**Cut rhythm:** Irregular but intentional. Not a metronomic beat. The cuts should feel motivated by the content, not by the music.

**The silence at 0:22 is non-negotiable.** It is the emotional pivot of the film. Do not cut into it early. Do not add music underneath it. Let it be uncomfortable for exactly the right amount of time.

**No transitions:** Not a single crossfade between scenes (except the end card fade). The film cuts. The viewer's eye adjusts. This is correct.

**Color grade:** 
- Slightly warm overall — push the highlights toward `#faf9f7`, the product's background color
- Desaturate slightly — this is not a colorful world
- Shadows: lifted, never crushed to black (except the intentional silence cut)
- The screen should read correctly — don't grade so warm that the UI whites shift

**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 native, designed for mobile viewing. If a horizontal cut is needed, shoot slightly wider and reframe in post.

## What This Film Is Not Allowed To Do

These are hard constraints. Not guidelines.

- **No voiceover.** The product speaks through use.
- **No feature callouts.** No text bubbles pointing at UI elements. No labels.
- **No speed-ramped montage sequence.** Every shot earns its place individually.
- **No reaction shots.** No employee smiling at the phone. No satisfaction expressed by a human face.
- **No stock footage.** Every frame is TimeKeep or the world it lives in.
- **No logo animation.** The wordmark appears once, at the end, without motion.
- **No percentage claims, user counts, or metrics.** Nothing quantified.
- **No competitor mentions or comparisons.**
- **No "intuitive," "seamless," "powerful," or "robust"** — in titles, voiceover, or description.

---

## The Standard

Every cut, every hold, every sound, every line of text should pass one test:

> *Does this serve the feeling of calm operational clarity?*

If the answer is uncertain, remove it.

The film is finished when nothing can be removed without losing something essential. Not when everything intended has been included.
