# TimeKeep — Portfolio Film

**Director's Document**

Runtime: 90–120 seconds  
Format: Screen Studio Capture Only  
Aspect Ratio: 16:9  
Tone: Intentional. Precise. Understated.

---

# The Brief

This film is not a product advertisement.

This film is not a feature walkthrough.

This film is evidence.

The audience is not a customer evaluating software.

The audience is:

- Engineering managers
- Recruiters
- Founders
- Technical leads

The goal is simple:

> Demonstrate product thinking, judgment, and execution.

Most software videos explain what the product does.

This film explains why it exists.

---

# Core Observation

TimeKeep was built around a simple observation:

> Small businesses do not need more software.
>
> They need fewer interruptions.

Most workforce management systems optimize for administrators.

TimeKeep optimizes for the employee standing in a workplace who needs one answer immediately.

Everything in the product follows from that constraint.

Everything in the film follows from that constraint.

# Visual Philosophy

Everything is captured using Screen Studio.

No stock footage.

No talking head.

No voiceover.

No feature callouts.

No cursor gimmicks.

No kinetic typography.

No startup marketing energy.

The interface is the subject.

Motion comes from interaction.

Typography appears rarely.

Every sentence must earn its place.

# Music Direction

Single ambient track.

No percussion.

No dramatic build.

No climax.

No drop.

The music should feel like breathing.

The software should feel like it belongs inside the soundtrack.

## References

- Ólafur Arnalds
- Nils Frahm
- Brian Eno
- Jon Hopkins (ambient work)

Reference the atmosphere.

Not the emotion.

---

# Editing Philosophy

Remove everything that feels like marketing.

Remove everything that feels like a tutorial.

Remove everything that feels like a startup pitch.

Keep only what demonstrates:

- Judgment
- Product thinking
- Restraint
- Execution

Every cut should answer:

> Does this improve understanding?

If not, remove it.

---

# Hard Constraints

The film is not allowed to contain:

- Voiceovers
- Feature callouts
- Animated arrows
- Cursor highlights
- Logo animations
- Statistics
- User counts
- Testimonials
- Competitor comparisons
- Buzzwords
- Startup language

Avoid words such as:

- Powerful
- Seamless
- Robust
- Revolutionary
- Intuitive

The software should demonstrate its quality.

The film should never claim it.

---

# The Standard

The viewer should finish the film with one impression:

> This person built software for real people and understood the tradeoffs involved.

Not because the film said so.

Because the product made it obvious.
